Reporting Requirements

Are there circumstances under which a physician is required by law to report a patient's condition, illness, injury or disease?

Yes. A physician is or may be required to make reports under certain circumstances concerning the following:

  • Abortions. See ABORTION.
  • AIDS, HIV, or STDs. See AIDS/HIV/STD.
  • Animal Bites. See ANIMAL BITES.
  • Assisted reproductions. See ASSISTED REPRODUCTION.
  • Births. See BIRTH CERTIFICATES.
  • Birth defects. See BIRTH DEFECTS.
  • Child abuse. See CHILD ABUSE.
  • Communicable diseases and outbreaks. See COMMUNICABLE DISEASES; and NOTIFIABLE CONDITIONS.
  • Deaths. See DEATH CERTIFICATES.
  • Vulnerable adult abuse. See VULNERABLE ADULT ABUSE.
  • Gunshot wounds. See GUNSHOT WOUNDS.
  • Pesticide poisonings. See PESTICIDE POISONING.
  • Phenylketonuria. See NEWBORNS: TESTING AND REPORTING.
  • Tuberculosis. See TUBERCULOSIS.
  • Other notifiable conditions. See NOTIFIABLE CONDITIONS.

Are there circumstances when a physician must report another physician's unprofessional conduct?

Yes. See MEDICAL DISCIPLINE.

Must a physician self report any information to the Medical Quality Assurance Commission?

Yes. See MEDICAL DISCIPLINE.

Must a physician report the theft or loss of controlled substances?

Yes. See CONTROLLED SUBSTANCES.
