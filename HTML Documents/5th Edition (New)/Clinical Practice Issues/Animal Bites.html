Animal Bites

Must a physician report cases of animal bites?

While not all animal bites require reporting, those bites in which human exposure to rabies is suspected is reportable to the local health department. 1 Principal health care providers should notify the local health department of animal bites immediately at the time of diagnosis or suspected diagnosis, by telephone or by secure facsimile transmission of a written case report. 2 If the latter method of communication is used, the party sending the report must confirm immediate receipt by a live person. 3 See NOTIFIABLE CONDITIONS.

See NOTIFIABLE CONDITIONS. 4

Are there any penalties for failing to report an animal bite case?

Failure to make a required report can constitute unprofessional conduct which may subject a physician to disciplinary action. 5 See UNPROFESSIONAL CONDUCT.
